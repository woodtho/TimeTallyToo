FAQ

Common questions

What is the Pomodoro technique? +

The Pomodoro technique is a time management method where you work in focused intervals (typically 25 minutes) separated by short breaks (5 minutes). After 4 work intervals, you take a longer break (15–30 minutes). TimeTally supports this pattern with any interval length you choose.

Does TimeTally support 25/5 Pomodoro? +

Yes. Add a 25-minute work task, a 5-minute break task, and repeat. TimeTally runs through the sequence automatically. The only difference from a basic Pomodoro timer is that you also see exactly when your whole session will end.

Is it free? +

Yes. Completely free — no account, no premium tier, no time limits. Every feature works immediately in your browser.

Can I save my Pomodoro routine? +

Yes. Your task lists save automatically in your browser. Install TimeTally as a PWA and your saved Pomodoro routine is one tap away every day — no login, no sync.
